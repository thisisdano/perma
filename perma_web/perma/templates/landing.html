Perma.cc prevents link rot.

Websites change, go away, and get taken down. When they do, the linked citations in your documents can lead to broken pages, blank pages, or places you never intended. This is called “link rot.” Perma.cc helps scholars, journals and courts prevent link rot by creating permanent, unalterable links to the online sources cited in their work. Here’s an example.

How Perma.cc works

1: Create

Convert your link into a Perma Link.

With a Perma.cc account, you can create links to permanent records of the web pages cited in your work. All you have to do is enter the URL of the page you want to preserve. Perma.cc creates a snapshot record of the page at that moment and gives you a unique, unbreakable Perma Link — like perma.cc/FC7M-CRS9 — that you can use in your citations to direct readers to the preserved record.

These unvested Perma Links remain active for two years. To preserve them permanently, continue to Step 2 and vest them.

More about creating Perma Links

2: Vest

Validate your Perma Link.

To keep your Perma Link active permanently, it needs to be “vested” by someone affiliated with a Vesting Organization, such as a library, journal, or court. Vesting assures that our Perma Links are valid, necessary, and correct. Perma.cc limits vesting privileges to help prevent abuse and to focus on solving the problem of link rot in academic and legal writing.

More about vesting and Vesting Organizations

3: Archive

Preserve your Perma Link permanently.

Once your Perma Link is vested, it will be preserved in perpetutity by our network of participating libraries. You can organize and manage your Perma Links through your Perma.cc Dashboard.

More about Perma Link records
